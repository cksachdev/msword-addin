The stopping distance of a vehicle is the total distance travelled from the moment

the driver sees the hazard to when the vehicle stops. Stopping distance consists of two parts:

 

(1) Reaction distance: the distance travelled before the driver actually starts applying brakes

Thus Stopping distance = Reaction distance + Braking distance.

 

Distance is calculated using the formula: x = ut where x is the distance in metres, u is the initial speed in metre/second and t is the time in seconds.

Question

 

In the 18th century, the French used a decimal clock to tell time. A day was divided into 10 hours, an hour was divided into 100 minutes and a minute was divided into 100 seconds.

 

Question .1

What time will the 24-hour clock show if the time on the decimal clock reads 3.50?

A. 08:40

B. 08:24

C. 05:48

D. 05:50

 

Question .2

How many days does a non-leap year have according to the decimal clock? Explain your answer.

 

Answer

 

Question .1: 08:24

Question .2: 365.25 (365 can be considered right)

There won’t be any change to the number of days in a year as the definition of the day does not change no matter what clock we use - we call the time that the earth takes to complete a rotation about its axis a day.
