The stopping distance of a vehicle is the total distance travelled from the moment  

the driver sees the hazard to when the vehicle stops. Stopping distance consists of two parts:  

 

(1) Reaction distance: the distance travelled before the driver actually starts applying brakes 

 

(2) Braking distance: the distance the vehicle travels after the brakes are applied and before the vehicle comes to rest 

 

Thus Stopping distance = Reaction distance + Braking distance. 

 

Distance is calculated using the formula: x = ut where x is the distance in metres, u is the initial speed in metre/second and t is the time in seconds.
